## A Problem I Care About

A problem I care deeply about is protecting vulnerable populations—especially aging individuals like my mother—from scams, robocalls, and social engineering attacks. As spam calls become more sophisticated, many people are targeted repeatedly and pressured into giving up sensitive information like banking details or personal data. This is not just an inconvenience; it is a real and growing threat that can cause financial harm and emotional distress.

Watching how frequently these calls reach people who may be more trusting or less familiar with modern fraud tactics has made this issue feel very personal to me. I think about how easily someone could be manipulated in the wrong moment, and it has made me more interested in creating guardrails that help protect people before damage is done.

From a customer experience and operations perspective, this problem also erodes trust in legitimate communication channels. When people can no longer trust phone calls, it becomes harder for real businesses to serve customers effectively. I’m especially interested in how technology, analytics, and better system design can be used to proactively identify threats, educate users, and create safer communication environments for everyone.

## How Generative AI Can Help

- **Intelligent Call Screening and Transcription Analysis:** Generative AI can analyze incoming call patterns, transcribe and interpret voicemails in real time, and flag suspicious language or known spam signatures — allowing users to screen calls before they ever answer, without missing legitimate communications.

- **Adaptive Spam Detection Models:** Unlike static blocklists, generative AI can continuously learn from new spam tactics, generating updated detection rules and response strategies as bad actors evolve their methods — staying ahead of the problem rather than reacting to it.

- **Personalized Caller Trust Scoring:** By synthesizing data from call history, contact metadata, and behavioral patterns, generative AI can assign dynamic trust scores to incoming numbers and provide users with context-aware recommendations, making it easier to decide what to answer, ignore, or report.
